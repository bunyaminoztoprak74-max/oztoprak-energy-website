# Oztoprak Energy Growth Plan

Website: https://www.oztoprakenerji.com  
Primary market: Turkey, with English support for international EPC, investor and owner-side engineering searches  
Core objective: turn the website into a focused organic traffic and lead generation platform for renewable energy consulting, power plant technical advisory and industrial electricity cost optimization.

## 1. Executive Summary

Oztoprak Energy already has a strong technical base: bilingual EN/TR routing, a sitemap index, service pages, project/case study content, blog clusters, schema infrastructure and a clean dark corporate visual identity. The biggest growth opportunity is not to publish more URLs blindly. The priority is to make Google trust a smaller group of high-value pages first, then expand programmatic SEO after the domain has stronger indexed coverage and engagement signals.

The current site can compete for renewable energy consulting keywords if the indexation strategy is tightened around:

- High-intent Turkish service pages.
- Real engineering case studies.
- Technical blog pillars that support service pages.
- Strong internal links from homepage, services, blog and footer.
- New landing pages for industrial electricity consumers.

Recommended direction:

1. Keep the sitemap selective and quality-first.
2. Push only the strongest commercial and authority pages for indexing first.
3. Build separate lead funnels for renewable asset owners and industrial electricity consumers.
4. Use bilingual pages, but prioritize Turkish for near-term commercial demand.
5. Add an Energy Cost Optimization service family because it can generate leads from factories and high-consumption facilities beyond renewable project owners.

## 2. SEO Structure Audit

### Current Strengths

- `robots.txt` allows important pages and references `https://www.oztoprakenerji.com/sitemap.xml`.
- Sitemap index is active and references:
  - `https://www.oztoprakenerji.com/page-sitemap.xml`
  - `https://www.oztoprakenerji.com/blog-sitemap.xml`
  - `https://www.oztoprakenerji.com/service-sitemap.xml`
- Canonical domain is aligned around `https://www.oztoprakenerji.com`.
- EN/TR hreflang exists across major pages.
- Service sitemap includes high-value consulting URLs.
- Blog and project content support topical authority.
- Schema systems exist for organization, services, articles, breadcrumbs and FAQs.
- Core Web Vitals appear healthy based on the latest PageSpeed screenshots: mobile performance around 96, desktop 100, SEO 100.

### Key SEO Risks

1. Too many URLs too early.

   Google Search Console previously showed hundreds of discovered but not indexed URLs. That is usually a quality, crawl priority and authority issue. It does not mean every URL is broken. It means Google does not yet see enough value or internal priority to index all of them.

2. Programmatic pages should be controlled.

   Topics, locations, pillars and generated pages should not all be pushed equally until the strongest service, case study and blog pages are indexed. Low-authority domains often struggle when hundreds of similar programmatic URLs appear before Google has enough trust signals.

3. Blog category hreflang should be reviewed.

   Live sitemap output suggests some blog category alternates may be paired by list order rather than exact semantic equivalent. Example risk: an English category may point to a Turkish category with a different topic. This should be fixed with explicit category mapping.

4. Future-dated `lastmod` values should be avoided.

   Some sitemap entries appear to use future dates. Google may ignore this, but it can reduce trust in sitemap freshness. Only use current or past publication/update dates.

5. Industrial electricity consumer intent is underdeveloped.

   The current website is strong for renewable project owners, EPC contractors and plant operators. It needs a dedicated pathway for factories and industrial consumers searching for electricity bill savings, reactive penalty issues, power quality, rooftop solar feasibility and energy cost optimization.

## 5. Lead Generation Strategy For Industrial Electricity Consumers

### Target Customers

Focus on facilities where energy cost is a board-level issue:

- Organized industrial zone factories.
- Textile, food, plastics, metal, cement and mining facilities.
- Cold storage and logistics facilities.
- Hotels, hospitals and large commercial buildings.
- Facilities with high reactive penalties.
- Facilities considering rooftop solar.
- Facilities with transformer capacity, contract power or power quality issues.

### Offer Ladder

1. Free lead magnet:

   "Industrial Electricity Bill Risk Scan"

   User submits one electricity bill and receives a short risk/opportunity review.

2. Entry-level paid service:

   "Industrial Energy Cost Diagnostic"

   Scope: bill analysis, contract power review, reactive penalty review, load profile review, compensation risk and savings estimate.

3. Technical advisory:

   "Power Quality and Reactive Penalty Review"

   Scope: field measurement plan, compensation panel review, harmonic risk, transformer loading, penalty elimination actions.

4. Investment advisory:

   "Rooftop Solar Feasibility and Self-Consumption Model"

   Scope: consumption baseline, roof suitability, generation estimate, self-consumption ratio, payback, grid/export assumptions.

5. Retainer:

   "Energy Cost Monitoring Advisory"

   Monthly review of consumption, penalties, production-normalized energy intensity and tariff/contract optimization.

### Funnel Design

Landing page flow:

1. Problem statement: high electricity cost, reactive penalties, unclear tariff/contract risks.
2. Trust proof: 28+ years power plant operations, EPC and technical advisory experience.
3. Diagnostic offer: free bill risk scan.
4. Form fields:
   - Name
   - Company
   - Email
   - Phone
   - Monthly electricity bill range
   - Facility type
   - Optional bill upload
5. CTA options:
   - Request Electricity Cost Review
   - Send Bill for Preliminary Analysis
   - Book Technical Assessment
6. Follow-up:
   - Auto response email.
   - WhatsApp/phone outreach.
   - Diagnostic memo.
   - Proposal for paid audit.

### Conversion Tracking

Track these events:

- Contact form submission.

## 9. Indexing Strategy

### Keep Indexable

- Homepage and localized homepage.
- Core service pages.
- Strong technical case studies.
- Strong long-form blog articles.
- Contact, about and projects pages.
- Select high-intent location and industry pages.

### Keep Out Of Sitemap Until Stronger

- Thin location pages.
- Weak topic pages.
- Low-value category pages.
- Programmatic pages with similar templates.
- Any page below 800-1000 words unless it has clear navigational purpose.

### Noindex Candidates

- Internal search pages.
- Tag pages with thin content.
- Duplicate category/archive pages.
- Programmatic pages created only for keyword variation.
- Utility pages not meant for search.

### Sitemap Principles

- Include only canonical HTTP 200 URLs.
- Do not include redirected URLs.
- Do not include noindex URLs.
- Do not use future `lastmod` dates.
- Use explicit hreflang mapping, especially for categories.
- Prioritize service and authority pages over mass-generated pages.
